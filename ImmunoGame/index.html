Q1. Act as a clinical immunologist tasked with diagnosing a patient with either Type 1 Diabetes OR Rheumatoid Arthritis. Write a comprehensive report that describes the immunopathobiology of your chosen disease and critically evaluates at least three common immunological tests used for its diagnosis.

Type 1 Diabetes Answer

Organ-specific autoimmune disease characterised by the breakdown of self-tolerance, leading to the destruction of insulin-producing pancreatic beta cells. Dendritic cells present autoantigens to naive T cells in pancreatic lymph nodes. CD4+ Th1 cells secrete inflammatory cytokines, activating macrophages and CD8+ cytotoxic T lymphocytes. CD8+ cells infiltrate the islets of Langerhans inducing apoptosis. B cells produce autoantibodies (biomarkers).

Rheumatoid Arthritis Answer

Systemic autoimmune disorder causing chronic joint inflammation. Loss of tolerance to citrullinated proteins forms immune complexes in synovial fluid (Type III hypersensitivity). Antigen-presenting cells activate CD4+ Th1/Th17 cells, secreting TNF-alpha and IL-6. Activates macrophages, fibroblasts, osteoclasts causing hyperplasia and bone erosion.

Q2(a). Discuss the pathophysiology of allergic disorders and critically evaluate the diagnostic tests targeted towards them.

Allergic Disorders Answer

Type I immediate hypersensitivity. Sensitisation phase: APCs present allergen to Th2 cells (IL-4, IL-13). B cells isotype switch to IgE. IgE binds high-affinity Fc-epsilon-RI on mast cells/basophils. Re-exposure cross-links IgE causing rapid degranulation (histamine/tryptase) causing vasodilation and bronchoconstriction.

Q2(b). With reference to a specific disorder of the complement system, describe a modern monoclonal antibody-based treatment and explain its mechanism of action.

Paroxysmal Nocturnal Haemoglobinuria (PNH) Answer

Severe complement system disorder causing RBC destruction. Mutation in PIG-A gene in stem cells stops synthesis of GPI anchors. RBCs cannot tether complement regulators CD55 and CD59 to membrane. Unregulated Membrane Attack Complex (MAC) causes lysis.
